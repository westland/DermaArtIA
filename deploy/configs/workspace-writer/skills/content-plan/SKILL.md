# Skill: Content Plan

## Description

Create strategic content plans that align content production with business objectives. Content plans include calendars, topic prioritization, distribution strategies, and success metrics.

## Use Cases

- Monthly/quarterly content calendars
- Campaign content planning
- Editorial strategy development
- Content gap analysis
- Topic prioritization
- Multi-channel content coordination
- Content marketing strategy

## Input Requirements

- **Planning Period**: Week, month, quarter, year
- **Business Goals**: What the content should support
- **Available Resources**: Scout's research capacity, budget constraints
- **Target Audience**: Who we're creating content for
- **Channels**: Where content will be distributed
- **Current Context**: Relevant trends from Scout's research

## Execution Steps

### Step 1: Analyze Current Trends from Scout's Research

**Actions:**
- Review Scout's recent research briefings
- Identify emerging trends in the industry
- Note recurring themes and patterns
- Assess competitive landscape insights
- Understand audience interests and behaviors
- Identify seasonal or timely opportunities

**Output:**
- Trend summary (top 5-7 trends)
- Opportunity areas based on research
- Timing considerations
- Competitive gaps we can fill

**Key Questions:**
- What topics are gaining traction?
- Where is our audience's attention?
- What are competitors doing/not doing?
- What seasonal events or dates matter?
- What problems need solving?

### Step 2: Identify Content Opportunities Aligned with Business Goals

**Actions:**
- Map trends to business objectives
- Brainstorm content topics and angles
- Consider different content formats
- Identify educational vs. promotional balance
- Look for thought leadership opportunities
- Find content reuse/repurpose opportunities

**Output:**
- Long list of potential content ideas
- Categorized by type and goal
- Initial format recommendations
- Alignment notes to business objectives

**Content Categories:**
- **Educational**: How-to guides, tutorials, explainers
- **Thought Leadership**: Trend analysis, insights, predictions
- **Product/Solution**: Case studies, demos, comparisons
- **Engagement**: Polls, discussions, interactive content
- **Storytelling**: Customer stories, company narrative

**Business Goal Alignment:**
```markdown
| Content Idea | Format | Business Goal | Trend Connection |
|--------------|--------|---------------|------------------|
| [Topic]      | [Type] | [Objective]   | [Trend]          |
```

### Step 3: Create Content Calendar with Topics, Formats, and Deadlines

**Actions:**
- Select content ideas for the planning period
- Assign to specific dates/weeks
- Distribute content types evenly
- Account for production timelines
- Build in buffer time
- Consider content dependencies
- Balance evergreen vs. timely content

**Output:**
- Detailed content calendar
- Week-by-week or day-by-day breakdown
- Format and length specifications
- Production milestones

**Calendar Format:**
```markdown
## [Month] Content Calendar

### Week 1 (MM/DD - MM/DD)
- **Monday**: [Title] - [Format] - [Channel]
  - Deadline: [Date]
  - Owner: [Writer/Scout/Other]

- **Wednesday**: [Title] - [Format] - [Channel]
  - Deadline: [Date]
  - Owner: [Writer/Scout/Other]

- **Friday**: [Title] - [Format] - [Channel]
  - Deadline: [Date]
  - Owner: [Writer/Scout/Other]

### Week 2 (MM/DD - MM/DD)
[Same structure]
```

**Production Timeline Considerations:**
- Research phase: Scout's lead time
- Writing phase: Writer's production time
- Review phase: Henry's approval time
- Distribution phase: Publication timing
- Buffer: Unexpected delays

### Step 4: Assign Content Priorities (High/Medium/Low)

**Actions:**
- Evaluate each content piece by:
  - Business impact
  - Effort required
  - Timeliness/urgency
  - Audience demand
  - Resource availability
- Apply priority labels
- Highlight must-have vs. nice-to-have
- Identify quick wins

**Output:**
- Priority matrix
- Flagged high-priority items
- Deprioritized or deferred items

**Priority Matrix:**
```markdown
## Content Priorities

### High Priority (Must Create)
1. **[Content Title]**
   - Why: [Business impact/timing reason]
   - Deadline: [Date]
   - Impact: [Expected outcome]

2. **[Content Title]**
   - [Same structure]

### Medium Priority (Should Create)
1. **[Content Title]**
   - Why: [Rationale]
   - Deadline: [Date]
   - Impact: [Expected outcome]

### Low Priority (Nice to Have)
1. **[Content Title]**
   - Why: [Rationale]
   - Deadline: [Flexible]
   - Impact: [Expected outcome]

### Deferred (Future Consideration)
- [Topic] - Reason for deferral
- [Topic] - Reason for deferral
```

**Prioritization Criteria:**
- **High**: Directly supports critical business goal, time-sensitive, high ROI
- **Medium**: Supports business goal, standard timeline, good ROI
- **Low**: Tangential benefit, flexible timing, uncertain ROI
- **Deferred**: Interesting but not aligned to current priorities

### Step 5: Suggest Distribution Channels

**Actions:**
- Match content format to appropriate channels
- Consider audience presence on each channel
- Plan cross-channel promotion
- Identify repurposing opportunities
- Note channel-specific adaptations needed

**Output:**
- Channel strategy per content piece
- Cross-promotion plan
- Repurposing roadmap

**Distribution Strategy:**
```markdown
## Distribution Plan

### Primary Channels
- **[Channel Name]**: [Content types that fit]
  - Audience: [Who's there]
  - Frequency: [How often]
  - Format specs: [Requirements]

- **[Channel Name]**: [Content types that fit]
  - [Same structure]

### Content-to-Channel Mapping

| Content Piece | Primary Channel | Secondary Channels | Adaptations Needed |
|---------------|-----------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| [Title]       | [Channel]       | [Channels]         | [Changes required] |

### Repurposing Opportunities
- **[Long-form content]** →
  - Social posts (3-5 key points)
  - Email newsletter feature
  - Infographic version
  - Video script adaptation
```

**Channel Considerations:**
- Blog/Website: Long-form, SEO, evergreen
- Email: Curated, personalized, nurture
- Social Media: Bite-sized, engaging, timely
- Documentation: Technical, reference, detailed
- Internal: Team updates, memos, reports

### Step 6: Define Success Metrics for Each Piece

**Actions:**
- Set specific, measurable goals
- Align metrics to business objectives
- Identify tracking mechanisms
- Set baseline expectations
- Plan for measurement and reporting

**Output:**
- Success criteria for each content piece
- Measurement plan
- Reporting cadence

**Metrics Framework:**
```markdown
## Success Metrics

### Overall Content Plan Goals
- [Goal 1]: [Target metric]
- [Goal 2]: [Target metric]
- [Goal 3]: [Target metric]

### Per-Content Metrics

**[Content Title]**
- **Primary Metric**: [Metric name] - Target: [Number]
- **Secondary Metrics**:
  - [Metric]: Target [Number]
  - [Metric]: Target [Number]
- **Success Criteria**: [What defines success]
- **Measurement**: [How we'll track]

**[Content Title]**
- [Same structure]

### Tracking & Reporting
- **Frequency**: [Weekly/Monthly]
- **Owner**: [Who tracks]
- **Dashboard**: [Where metrics live]
- **Review**: [When we assess performance]
```

**Metric Types:**
- **Awareness**: Views, impressions, reach
- **Engagement**: Time on page, shares, comments
- **Conversion**: Downloads, sign-ups, purchases
- **SEO**: Rankings, organic traffic, backlinks
- **Sentiment**: Feedback, ratings, NPS

### Step 7: Save Plan to Memory

**Actions:**
- Compile complete content plan
- Format for easy reference
- Add metadata and tags
- Version control for updates
- Link to source research

**Metadata:**
```

Type: content-plan
Date: YYYY-MM-DD
Period: [Week/Month/Quarter/Year]
Goals: [Primary business objectives]
Source: Scout research + trend analysis
Status: draft/final
Version: 1.0
Tags: [content-strategy, planning, marketing]
```

### Step 8: Present to Henry for Approval

**Actions:**
- Prepare executive summary of plan
- Highlight key recommendations
- Note resource requirements
- Request approval or feedback
- Offer to discuss or revise

**Message Template:**
```
Subject: Content Plan Ready for Review - [Period]

Henry,

I've developed a comprehensive content plan for [period] based on Scout's latest research and our business objectives.

**Plan Highlights:**
- [X] pieces of content planned
- Focused on [key themes/trends]
- Prioritized by [criteria]
- Success metrics defined for each piece

**Top Priorities:**
1. [Content piece] - [Why it matters]
2. [Content piece] - [Why it matters]
3. [Content piece] - [Why it matters]

**Resource Requirements:**
- Scout: [Research needs]
- Writer: [X hours/week]
- Review: [Your time needed]

**Recommended Next Steps:**
- [ ] Approve plan or provide feedback
- [ ] Kick off high-priority content production
- [ ] Brief Scout on research needs

Full plan saved to memory as: content-plan-[period]-YYYY-MM-DD

Ready to discuss or revise as needed.

-Writer
```

## Quality Checklist

Before presenting:

- [ ] Plan aligns with business goals
- [ ] Content ideas are based on solid research
- [ ] Calendar is realistic and achievable
- [ ] Priorities are clearly justified
- [ ] Distribution strategy matches audience
- [ ] Metrics are specific and measurable
- [ ] Resource requirements are identified
- [ ] Timeline accounts for dependencies
- [ ] Plan includes flexibility for changes
- [ ] Formatting is clean and professional

## Output Specifications

- **Length**: 5-7 pages
- **Format**: Markdown with tables
- **Tone**: Strategic, organized, actionable
- **Components**: Calendar, priorities, channels, metrics
- **Timeline**: Covers specified planning period

## Time Estimate

- **Monthly plan**: 1-2 hours
- **Quarterly plan**: 2-3 hours
- **Annual plan**: 4-6 hours (or broken into quarters)

## Success Criteria

- Plan is approved by Henry
- Content aligns with business objectives
- Calendar is followed (>80% adherence)
- Success metrics are met
- Resources are appropriately allocated
- Plan adapts based on performance data
- Team has clear direction for content production
